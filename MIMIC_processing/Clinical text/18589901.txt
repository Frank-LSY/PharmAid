Post-Operative Instructions 
Endoscopic Sinus Surgery

An Endoscopic Sinus Surgery is an operation to open up the 
sinuses and remove disease from them with the help of 
telescopes.

       You may have clear packing in your nose after the 
surgery which may fall out like a clear piece of plastic in the 
next few days. In all cases, expect congested nasal breathing to 
some degree in the first few weeks. 

       Do not blow your nose. If you sneeze, do so with your 
mouth open.

       Expect some bloody discharge or blood-tinged secretions. 
Bright red blood is not expected, and should not be seen.

       Do not use aspirin or any non-steroidal medicines (like 
Motrin, Advil, ibuprofen), as these may increase bleeding.

       Pain will be assessed, and medication prescribed, before 
you leave the hospital.

       Start your antibiotics and any nasal sprays the night of 
the surgery.

       Start the saline irrigations the day after surgery

       Keep your follow-up appointment.
